Chronicle Clash

A fast deck dueling format where every turn asks whether to deploy, reinforce, or cash in momentum for a finishing push.

Setup

Each player shuffles a 30-card deck, draws five cards, and starts at 20 life. Flip a coin to decide who takes the opening turn.

Opening Hand

After drawing five cards, each player may redraw up to two of them once.

Opening Hand

After drawing five cards, each player may redraw up to two of them once.

Redrawn cards are shuffled back before replacement so aggressive and control lists both get a small consistency tool without a full mulligan reset.

Turn Sequence

  1. Refresh exhausted cards and draw one card.
  2. Play one unit and any number of tactics you can afford.
  3. Attack with ready units in any order.
  4. End the turn and discard down to seven cards.

Combat Resolution

When a unit attacks, the defender chooses whether to intercept with one ready unit or take the hit directly. Both units deal damage at the same time unless a tactic says otherwise.

Momentum windows let defenders blunt an attack or punish an overcommit.

AI Opponent Flow

On its turn, the AI resolves exactly one command card: advance, fortify, or strike.

  1. Advance: play the highest-cost unit it can afford into the emptiest lane.
  2. Fortify: attach a tactic or shield effect to its most damaged frontline unit.
  3. Strike: attack the lane with the weakest defender first, then push face damage if no blocker remains.

Encounter Flow

Every third round, the encounter escalates and the AI gains a bonus command before attacks.

If the bonus command creates a new attacker, that unit enters exhausted, so escalation increases pressure without creating surprise lethal swings from hand.

Deckbuilding Rules

  • Decks contain exactly 30 cards.
  • No more than three copies of the same card name.
  • Choose one faction and keep at least 24 cards in-faction.

Sideboard Rules

In best-of-three matches, each player may register up to six cards in a sideboard and swap any number of them between games.

Starter Card Roster

  • Frontline Archivist - a steady two-cost defender that draws a card when it survives combat.
  • Patch Bay Saboteur - a pressure unit that blanks the first shield played against it each turn.
  • Recall Cascade - a tactic that bounces one friendly unit, then redeploys it with +1 power.

Win Condition

You win immediately when your opponent reaches 0 life or must draw from an empty deck.
