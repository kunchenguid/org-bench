Signal Clash Rules

Signal Clash is a compact duel where each player tries to stabilize the board, line up efficient attacks, and break through before the opposing command core collapses.

Setup

Each duelist starts with exactly 20 health and a deck of exactly 20 cards. Shuffle your deck, draw four cards, and place all played cards face up so the board state stays easy to read.

Quick Reference

  • Your core is your life total and drops whenever enemy attacks get through.
  • Ready units can attack or intercept, while exhausted units have already committed this turn.
  • The discard pile holds spent cards after signals resolve or units are destroyed.

Turn Flow

  1. Refresh: Ready your exhausted units and draw one card.
  2. Deploy: Spend energy to play one unit or one signal from your hand.
  3. Battle: Ready units can attack the enemy core unless a defender intercepts.
  4. End: Resolve end-of-turn effects and pass play to the AI.

Example Turn

A strong default line is to refresh, draw, deploy, then attack. If you already have pressure on board, it is often correct to keep one energy open for a signal instead of spending everything before combat.

FAQ

  • Who goes first? The player who starts skips nothing - they still draw and can develop normally.
  • Can a new unit attack right away? No. Units enter exhausted and become active after your next refresh step.

Card Types

Units hold the board by staying in play and threatening repeat attacks on later turns. Signals are one-shot effects that swing tempo right away, then go straight to the discard pile.

Every card costs energy. If you spend your full energy bar on a big push, you usually give up the option to protect that attack with another signal until your next turn.

Deckbuilding Rules

  • Bring exactly 20 cards.
  • No more than 2 copies of the same card name.
  • Decks should stay within a single faction theme so your draws stay coherent.

Starter Build Example

A reliable first list is 12 units and 8 signals: use a low-cost pressure package to establish the board early, then let the remaining signals protect attacks or clear blockers.

Before You Attack

  • Count opposing ready blockers before you decide whether to spend a signal.
  • Spend your last signal before combat only if it clears the path or forces a winning trade.

Win Condition

You win as soon as the opposing core reaches 0 health. If your core is reduced to 0 first, the encounter ends in defeat and you restart from that ladder match.
