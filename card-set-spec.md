# Prism Duel Card Set

## Premise

The duel takes place at the Prism Well, a living reservoir of refracted light at the center of a drowned observatory. The player defends the well as the Sol Covenant, an order of lantern-bearers and oathsworn guardians who stabilize the light into wards, banners, and disciplined formations. The enemy is the Umbral Court, a tide of nightglass seers and grave-raised wardens trying to corrupt the well and drink its stored radiance.

This gives the match a clear story even without a splash screen: hold the well against the first Umbral breach.

## Hero Concepts

### Player Hero

- Name: Astra, Keeper of the Third Ray
- Role: commander of the Sol Covenant defense
- Read on board: resilient, disciplined, upright, warm-gold highlights
- Gameplay identity: build a board, protect key units, convert a stable formation into a tempo swing

### Enemy Hero

- Name: Morrow, Regent Beneath the Glass Tide
- Role: leader of the Umbral Court incursion
- Read on board: elegant, predatory, violet-blue shadows, fractured halo
- Gameplay identity: chip life totals, pick off units, win through attrition and opportunistic bursts

## Factions

### Sol Covenant

- Theme: lantern banners, oathstone armor, noonlight geometry
- Emotional read: hopeful, steady, protective
- Deck identity: establish the board, force awkward attacks into Guards, then convert width into pressure with a formation buff
- Core decisions: when to spend removal versus add to board, when to protect a Scribe versus push damage, when to hold Banner until it swings multiple lanes

### Umbral Court

- Theme: moonlit glass, drowned silk, ceremonial decay
- Emotional read: patient, invasive, draining
- Deck identity: trade resources efficiently, heal through chip damage, and break parity with removal and life drain
- Core decisions: whether to spend damage on units or face, whether to stabilize behind a Warden or race with evasive pressure, when to turn a small advantage into lethal

## Mechanics

Keep the mechanic count low and readable. Every card should ladder into one of these.

1. Guard - must be answered before attacks can hit the hero.
2. Swift - can attack the turn it enters.
3. Insight - draw a card when summoned.
4. Formation Buff - a spell that rewards already having units on board.
5. Drain - damage that also heals the casting hero.

## Card Pool

Twelve unique cards is enough. Every card earns a seat by serving the identity of its faction.

### Sol Covenant Cards

1. Lantern Squire
Cost 1, Unit, 1/2
Rules: Simple early body that contests the board and carries Banner buffs well.

2. Bastion Halberdier
Cost 2, Unit, 1/4, Guard
Rules: The anchor card. Protects fragile value units and buys time for formation play.

3. Raycharge Lancer
Cost 3, Unit, 3/2, Swift
Rules: The tempo finisher. Converts a lead into immediate pressure or picks off a damaged blocker.

4. Prism Archivist
Cost 2, Unit, 2/3, Insight
Rules: Smooths draws and rewards the player for protecting value instead of overtrading.

5. Sunlance Volley
Cost 2, Spell
Rules: Deal 2 damage to an enemy unit or, if the lane is clear, the enemy hero. Flexible stabilizer that still allows reach.

6. Banner of Noon
Cost 3, Spell
Rules: Give your board +1/+1. This is the payoff for assembling a stable front line instead of dumping damage too early.

### Umbral Court Cards

1. Gloam Prowler
Cost 1, Unit, 2/1
Rules: High-pressure opener that asks whether the defender spends removal now or accepts chip damage.

2. Grave Warden
Cost 2, Unit, 1/5, Guard
Rules: The attrition wall. Forces inefficient attacks and makes Drain windows matter.

3. Dusk Harrier
Cost 3, Unit, 3/2, Swift
Rules: Mirrors the Sol finisher but feels hungrier and more opportunistic. Punishes weak positioning.

4. Wake Seer
Cost 2, Unit, 2/2, Insight
Rules: Keeps the Umbral hand stocked so the deck can continue trading and draining.

5. Soul Siphon
Cost 2, Spell
Rules: Deal 2 to the enemy hero and heal 2. Defines the Court's inevitability pattern.

6. Eclipse Hex
Cost 3, Spell
Rules: Deal 3 damage to an enemy unit. Clean answer to a buff target or Guard that would otherwise lock the lane.

## Deck Construction

Both decks use twenty cards with repeats, but the repeat pattern should feel deliberate.

### Sol Covenant List

- 4x Lantern Squire
- 4x Bastion Halberdier
- 3x Prism Archivist
- 3x Raycharge Lancer
- 3x Sunlance Volley
- 3x Banner of Noon

This list says: survive the first exchanges, keep enough bodies alive, then turn one stable board into a decisive swing.

### Umbral Court List

- 4x Gloam Prowler
- 4x Grave Warden
- 3x Wake Seer
- 3x Dusk Harrier
- 3x Soul Siphon
- 3x Eclipse Hex

This list says: pressure early, force awkward blocks, then keep the defender off-balance with removal and life drain.

## Why This Set Creates Real Decisions

- Early turns matter because both factions have legitimate 1-cost plays with different incentives.
- Guard creates visible lane tension and makes attack choices legible to a first-time player.
- The two Swift units turn damage races into real tempo puzzles instead of static trading.
- The two draw units reward protecting value, not just sending everything forward.
- Banner of Noon asks Sol to delay gratification for a wider payoff.
- Soul Siphon and Eclipse Hex let Umbral choose between pressure and stabilization.

## Naming Bar

Names should stay concrete and ceremonial, not generic fantasy sludge. Good names in this world feel like military or court titles filtered through light and glass.

- Good: Banner of Noon, Grave Warden, Prism Archivist, Regent Beneath the Glass Tide
- Avoid: Fire Mage, Dark Knight, Light Warrior, Shadow Blast

## On-Board Framing

The board copy should reinforce the world in a few short beats only.

- Match title: Prism Duel
- Encounter subtitle: Hold the Prism Well
- Player prompt: Steady the line, then turn the light outward.
- Enemy prompt: The Court advances through fracture and hunger.
